# Cadillac EXT-E: Vehicle Design Brief

## 1. Program Vision & Mission

**Vision:** To establish Cadillac as the undisputed leader in the luxury electric truck segment by creating a vehicle that redefines performance, capability, and technological innovation.

**Mission:** To engineer and launch the Cadillac EXT-E, an all-electric supertruck that leverages General Motors' Ultium Platform to deliver class-leading power, range, and a signature Cadillac luxury experience.

## 2. Core Product Pillars

*   **Dominant Performance:** The EXT-E will deliver breathtaking acceleration and raw power, setting a new benchmark for electric trucks.
*   **Uncompromising Luxury:** A technologically advanced, handcrafted interior will provide a sanctuary of comfort and connectivity.
*   **Bold, Futuristic Design:** The design will be an evolution of the EXT-V's aggressive styling, adapted for an electric future.
*   **Advanced Technology:** The vehicle will serve as a showcase for GM's most advanced EV and autonomous driving technologies.

## 3. Key Performance Targets & Specifications

| Metric                | Target                                       | Notes                                                 |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Powertrain**        | Triple-Motor, All-Wheel Drive (eAWD)         | Two motors on rear axle, one on front                 |
| **Battery**           | 200 kWh Ultium Battery Pack (2-layer)        | 800V architecture for ultra-fast charging             |
| **Horsepower**        | ~830 HP                                      | "Watts to Freedom" / "V-Mode" launch control enabled  |
| **Torque**            | ~1,000 lb-ft (Motor)                         | Final wheel torque TBD via gear reduction             |
| **0-60 mph**          | < 3.5 seconds                                |                                                       |
| **Range (EPA Est.)**  | 400+ miles                                   |                                                       |
| **Charging**          | Up to 350 kW DC Fast Charging                | Adds ~100 miles of range in 10 minutes                |
| **Towing Capacity**   | 10,000+ lbs                                  |                                                       |
| **Air Suspension**    | 4-Corner Adaptive Air Ride                   | Up to 6 inches of height adjustment                   |
| **CrabWalk Mode**     | 4-Wheel Steer                                | For enhanced low-speed maneuverability                |

## 4. Design Language

*   **Exterior:**
    *   Maintains the powerful, wide stance of the EXT-V.
    *   **Sealed "Grille":** A gloss black, sealed front panel featuring intricate micro-etched patterns and a choreographed LED light signature that activates on approach.
    *   **Aerodynamic Enhancements:** Integrated air curtains in the front fascia, a functional hood vent for cooling, and an active aero spoiler at the rear of the cab.
    *   **Lighting:** Thinner, more vertically-stacked LED headlights and a full-width rear light bar.
    *   **Wheels:** Unique 24-inch aero-optimized wheel designs.

*   **Interior:**
    *   **Display:** Retains the 38-inch curved OLED display from the EXT-V, now with custom EV-centric graphics and energy flow visualizations.
    *   **Materials:** Sustainable luxury materials, including open-pore wood, recycled fabrics, and high-grade metals.
    *   **Center Console:** A redesigned "floating" center console to create a more open, airy cabin feel.

## 5. Market Positioning

*   **Primary Competitors:** GMC Hummer EV, Rivian R1T, Tesla Cybertruck, Ford F-150 Lightning (Platinum).
*   **Target Customer:** Affluent, tech-savvy early adopters who demand the ultimate in performance and luxury without compromising on sustainability or capability.
*   **Pricing:** Positioned at the premium end of the market, starting above the top-tier Hummer EV. Estimated MSRP: $120,000 - $140,000.

## 6. Program Timeline (Preliminary)

*   **Phase 1 (Design & Engineering):** Q1-Q3 2026
*   **Phase 2 (Mule & Prototype Testing):** Q4 2026 - Q2 2027
*   **Phase 3 (Pre-Production & Validation):** Q3 2027
*   **Phase 4 (Start of Production):** Q4 2027
*   **Public Reveal:** Detroit Auto Show 2027
