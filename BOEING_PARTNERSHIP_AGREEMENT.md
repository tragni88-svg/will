# Strategic Manufacturing Partnership: Project Icarus

**PARTIES:**
1.  **Wardenclyffe USA** (Technology Licensor)
2.  **The Boeing Company** (Prime Contractor / Manufacturer)

**SUBJECT:** Mass Production of Graviton Displacement Drive (GDD) Platforms for US Defense

## 1. Agreement Overview
Wardenclyffe USA selects **The Boeing Company** as the exclusive aerospace defense contractor to manufacture the **Icarus-V "Vanguard"** and **Icarus-H "Sky-Lifter"** platforms for the United States Department of Defense. This partnership leverages Boeing's industrial scale and defense integration experience with Wardenclyffe's breakthrough propulsion technology.

## 2. Scope of Work

### 2.1. Boeing Responsibilities
*   **Airframe Fabrication:** Utilization of the St. Louis and Everett facilities to produce the Carbon-Tri-Titanium hulls.
*   **Systems Integration:** Integration of US military avionics, Helios Weapon Systems, and secure communications gear.
*   **Final Assembly:** Assembly of the Graviton Displacement Cores (GDC) supplied by Wardenclyffe into the airframes.
*   **Testing:** Conducting flightworthiness certification in coordination with the US Space Force.

### 2.2. Wardenclyffe Responsibilities
*   **Core Supply:** Exclusive supplier of the GDC (Graviton Displacement Core) and Quantum Gyroscopes.
*   **Tech Transfer:** Providing "Black Box" integration kits to Boeing assembly lines (Boeing engineers will not have access to the internal physics of the Core).
*   **QA Oversight:** Maintaining a permanent quality assurance team at all Boeing production sites.

## 3. Production Targets

*   **Year 1:** 50 Vanguard Units (Space Superiority Fighters).
*   **Year 2:** 200 Vanguard Units + 50 Sky-Lifter Units (Heavy Transport).
*   **Year 5:** Full fleet replacement of legacy fighter jets (F-35/F-22).

## 4. Security Protocols
*   All Boeing manufacturing cells dedicated to Project Icarus will be designated **SCIFs (Sensitive Compartmented Information Facilities)**.
*   Workforce must hold Top Secret / SCI clearance.
