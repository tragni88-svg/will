# Gigafactory Network: Site Selection Criteria

## 1.0 Overview

This document defines the critical criteria for the selection of sites for the four Wardenclyffe Gigafactories in Texas, Ohio, Nevada, and South Carolina. The goal is to identify locations that offer the optimal balance of logistical efficiency, workforce availability, robust infrastructure, and favorable economic conditions. Each candidate site will be scored against the following criteria.

## 2.0 Logistics & Transportation Infrastructure

*   **Proximity to Major Transportation Hubs:** Must be within 50 miles of a major highway intersection and within 100 miles of a cargo-capable airport and rail spur.
*   **Supply Chain Access:** Proximity to key suppliers, including port access for international freight.
*   **Site Accessibility:** Adequate road infrastructure to support heavy truck traffic for construction and operations.

## 3.0 Workforce & Talent Availability

*   **Skilled Labor Pool:** A metropolitan statistical area (MSA) with a population of at least 1 million, with a demonstrated pool of skilled manufacturing, engineering, and technical talent.
*   **Educational Partnerships:** Proximity to universities and technical colleges for workforce development programs and R&D partnerships.
*   **Cost of Labor:** Competitive wage and benefits environment.

## 4.0 Utilities & Site Infrastructure

*   **Energy:** Access to a high-capacity, reliable power grid with redundant feeds. Preference for sites with access to renewable energy sources.
*   **Water & Wastewater:** Sufficient water supply and wastewater treatment capacity for large-scale industrial use.
*   **Telecommunications:** Access to high-speed fiber optic connectivity.
*   **Land:** A contiguous parcel of at least 1,000 acres, zoned for industrial use, with geotechnical stability.

## 5.0 Financial Incentives & Regulatory Environment

*   **State & Local Incentives:** A comprehensive package of tax abatements, grants, and other financial incentives valued at a minimum of 10% of the capital investment.
*   **Regulatory Stability:** A predictable and efficient permitting and regulatory process.
*   **Right-to-Work State:** Preference for locations in right-to-work states.

## 6.0 Community & Environmental Impact

*   **Community Support:** A positive and supportive relationship with the local community and government.
*   **Environmental Diligence:** The site must have a clean environmental record with no significant remediation requirements.
*   **Quality of Life:** A high quality of life for employees, with access to housing, healthcare, and educational facilities.
