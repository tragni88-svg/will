# Wardenclyffe Guild - Online Training Curriculum

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-01-27
**Author:** Gemini

---

### **Module 1: The Physics of Wireless Power**

*   **Objective:** To provide a foundational understanding of the science behind the Wardenclyffe grid.
*   **Topics:**
    *   1.1: A Brief History: From Nikola Tesla to Modern Resonance.
    *   1.2: Understanding Inductive Coupling and Resonant Inductive Coupling.
    *   1.3: Key Concepts: Frequency (60 kHz), Magnetic Fields, and Efficiency.
    *   1.4: The Role of Metamaterials and Fractal Antennas in the RRM-1.
    *   1.5: Safety: Why the Wardenclyffe grid's non-ionizing fields are safe.

---

### **Module 2: RRM-1 Technical Deep Dive**

*   **Objective:** To familiarize installers with the hardware they will be working with.
*   **Topics:**
    *   2.1: RRM-1 Unboxing and Component Identification (NVIDIA AI Sync Chip, Schneider Power Module, BASF Housing).
    *   2.2: Understanding the I/O: Power input, data port, and LED status indicators.
    *   2.3: The IP67-rated Housing: Sealing, mounting points, and heat dissipation.
    *   2.4: Device-Level Intelligence: How the onboard AI chip optimizes power reception.
    *   2.5: Handling and Storage Best Practices.

---

### **Module 3: Site Assessment & Pre-Installation Checklist**

*   **Objective:** To ensure every installation site is properly prepared for a successful outcome.
*   **Topics:**
    *   3.1: Locating the Main Service Panel and Identifying a suitable breaker.
    *   3.2: Performing a Load Calculation to ensure sufficient panel capacity.
    *   3.3: Identifying the optimal mounting location for the RRM-1 (interior vs. exterior).
    *   3.4: The "Digital Handshake": Using the Guild mobile app to confirm local grid signal strength.
    *   3.5: Customer Communication: Explaining the process to the homeowner.

---

### **Module 4: Standard Installation Procedure**

*   **Objective:** To provide a step-by-step guide for a perfect, repeatable installation.
*   **Topics:**
    *   4.1: Safety First: De-energizing the main panel and using proper PPE.
    *   4.2: Mounting the RRM-1 bracket and securing the device.
    *   4.3: Running conduit and wiring to the service panel.
    *   4.4: Making the final connection to a dedicated 2-pole breaker.
    *   4.5: Tidy and Professional: Wire management and site cleanup.

---

### **Module 5: System Activation & Commissioning**

*   **Objective:** To guide the installer through bringing the RRM-1 online and verifying its operation.
*   **Topics:**
    *   5.1: The Power-Up Sequence: Re-energizing the panel and observing the RRM-1's boot process.
    *   5.2: Interpreting the LED Status Lights (e.g., Searching, Syncing, Online).
    *   5.3: Using the Guild mobile app to officially commission the device.
    *   5.4: The Final Handover: Demonstrating the live system to the homeowner via their new app.

---

### **Module 6: Safety Protocols & Troubleshooting**

*   **Objective:** To prepare installers for common issues and reinforce safety procedures.
*   **Topics:**
    *   6.1: Advanced Safety: Lockout/Tagout procedures for complex scenarios.
    *   6.2: Troubleshooting "No Signal" errors.
    *   6.3: Diagnosing RRM-1 fault codes via the LED indicator.
    *   6.4: Escalation Procedures: When and how to contact Guild Partner Support.

---

### **Module 7: The Wardenclyffe Customer Experience**

*   **Objective:** To train installers to be excellent ambassadors for the Wardenclyffe brand.
*   **Topics:**
    *   7.1: Professionalism and Communication from arrival to departure.
    *   7.2: Answering Common Homeowner Questions (e.g., "Is it safe?", "How much will I save?").
    *   7.3: Walking the customer through the Wardenclyffe Home mobile app.
    *   7.4: The Importance of the Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) survey.

---

### **Module 8: Final Certification Exam**

*   **Objective:** To validate the knowledge gained throughout the online curriculum.
*   **Format:** A 100-question, multiple-choice online exam.
*   **Passing Score:** 90% or higher.
*   **Attempts:** Three attempts are permitted before requiring a manual review with a Guild instructor.
