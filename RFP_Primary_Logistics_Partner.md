# Request for Proposal (RFP)

**Project Title:** Primary Logistics Partner for the Wardenclyffe Initiative
**RFP Issue Date:** 2026-01-27
**Company:** Wardenclyffe USA

## 1.0 Introduction & Project Overview

Wardenclyffe USA is undertaking a historic infrastructure project, the "Wardenclyffe Initiative," to deploy a national wireless power grid. A key component of this initiative is the manufacturing and distribution of 140 million Residential Receiver Modules (RRM-1).

Production will be centered at four new US-based Gigafactories located in Texas, Ohio, Nevada, and South Carolina. Each factory will ramp to a full production capacity of 35 million units per year.

Wardenclyffe USA is seeking a world-class, long-term primary logistics partner to manage the transportation of all finished goods from these four manufacturing sites to a network of approximately 50 Metropolitan Distribution Centers (MDCs) across the contiguous United States.

This RFP invites qualified logistics providers to submit proposals for this multi-year, high-volume logistics operation.

## 2.0 Scope of Work

The selected partner will be responsible for the following core services:

*   **Transportation Management:**
    *   Provide and manage dedicated truckload (TL) and less-than-truckload (LTL) freight services from the four Gigafactory locations.
    *   Ensure consistent, on-time pickup and delivery to all designated MDCs.
    *   Manage a projected annual volume ramping to 140 million units, packaged in bulk shipping containers.
*   **Network Management:**
    *   Coordinate logistics across the four source points (TX, OH, NV, SC) and ~50 destination MDCs.
    *   Develop and optimize shipping routes for efficiency and cost-effectiveness.
*   **Technology & Visibility:**
    *   Provide a robust Transportation Management System (TMS).
    *   Offer real-time, end-to-end visibility for all shipments, accessible via a central dashboard or API.
    *   Integrate with Wardenclyffe's inventory management and factory output systems.
*   **Performance & Reporting:**
    *   Develop and report on Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), including on-time performance, cost-per-unit-shipped, transit time, and carrier performance.
    *   Participate in quarterly business reviews to drive continuous improvement.
*   **Scalability:**
    *   Demonstrate the capacity to scale operations in line with the phased ramp-up of the Gigafactory network over a 3-4 year period.

## 3.0 Proposal Submission Requirements

Respondents must structure their proposals to include the following sections:

1.  **Company Profile:** An overview of your company, its history, financial stability, and experience in managing large-scale, complex national supply chains.
2.  **Operational Plan:** A detailed description of your proposed solution, including:
    *   Carrier network strategy (e.g., dedicated fleet, brokerage, mix).
    *   Plan for managing the four distinct factory source points.
    *   Contingency plans for disruptions (e.g., weather, capacity surges).
3.  **Technology Platform:** A comprehensive overview of the TMS and visibility tools you will deploy. Include details on integration capabilities (EDI/API).
4.  **Pricing Structure:** A detailed pricing proposal. This should include, but is not limited to:
    *   Cost-per-mile estimates.
    *   Fuel surcharge program.
    *   Volume-based discount tiers.
    *   Any proposed technology or management fees.
5.  **Implementation Plan:** A timeline for onboarding, systems integration, and the launch of initial operations, aligning with our first factory output in Year 3.
6.  **Relevant Case Studies:** A minimum of two case studies detailing your experience with projects of similar scope and complexity.

## 4.0 Evaluation Criteria

Proposals will be evaluated based on the following criteria, weighted accordingly:

*   **Cost Competitiveness & Overall Value (35%)**
*   **Proven Experience & Past Performance in High-Volume Logistics (30%)**
*   **Technology & Data Integration Capabilities (20%)**
*   **Scalability & Flexibility of Proposed Solution (15%)**
